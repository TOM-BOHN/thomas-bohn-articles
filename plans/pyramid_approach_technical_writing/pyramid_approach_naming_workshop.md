# Name Workshop: Pyramid Approach Article & Framework

**Purpose:** Choose a final title for the article (and optionally the framework) that fits how you think about it and works for readers.  
**Current title:** *The Pyramid Approach to Technical Writing: Decomposition, Reuse, and AI Grounding*  
**Your own words (from interview):** "Framework-grounded writing"; decompose strategic asks into grounding components; build the story from raw materials. You don't really call it "pyramid style writing" in your head.

---

## What the name should do

- **Signal the idea:** Four levels, reuse, AI grounding, decomposition.
- **Be memorable:** One phrase readers can repeat ("the Pyramid approach" vs "framework-grounded writing").
- **Fit the audience:** PMs, architects, technical designers—people who get "decomposition" and "ground truth."
- **Optional:** Align with how you actually think about it (grounding, raw materials) so the title doesn't feel like a label you don't use.

---

## Option A: Keep current (Pyramid-led)

**The Pyramid Approach to Technical Writing: Decomposition, Reuse, and AI Grounding**

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Clear visual metaphor; "Pyramid" is sticky. | "Pyramid" isn't what you call it in your head. |
| Subtitle already carries decomposition, reuse, AI. | Long; "approach" is a bit generic. |
| Matches C4 parallel (four levels, zoom). | |

**Verdict:** Strong for *readers* (visual, scannable). You can still say "framework-grounded writing" in the intro and subtitle.

---

## Option B: Lead with framework-grounded (your language)

**Framework-Grounded Technical Writing: The Pyramid of Reuse and AI Grounding**

or shorter:

**Framework-Grounded Writing: Decomposition, Reuse, and AI**

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Uses the phrase you actually use. | "Framework-grounded" is less instantly visual than "Pyramid." |
| "Grounding" ties to AI/RAG and your metaphor. | May need one more beat to explain the four levels. |
| Distinct; fewer people use this exact phrase. | |

**Verdict:** Best fit for *your* mental model. Pyramid can stay as the main diagram and section name ("The Pyramid Framework").

---

## Option C: Decomposition-first (engineering angle)

**Decomposition-First Technical Writing: A Four-Level Pyramid for Reuse and AI Grounding**

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Speaks directly to engineers/architects. | "Decomposition-first" can sound process-heavy. |
| Mirrors "test-first," "documentation-first." | Less emphasis on "grounding" / your framing. |
| Pyramid still in subtitle for the visual. | |

**Verdict:** Good if you want to lean into the software-engineering audience; slightly less aligned with "framework-grounded."

---

## Option D: Document Pyramid (shape-first, short)

**The Document Pyramid: Technical Writing for Reuse and AI Grounding**

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Short, memorable, visual. | "Document" is broad; doesn't say "decomposition" or "framework." |
| "Pyramid" does the heavy lifting. | Could feel like a generic "layered docs" idea. |
| Subtitle can carry decomposition + AI. | |

**Verdict:** Good for a punchy headline; you'd rely on the subtitle and opening to add "framework-grounded" and decomposition.

---

## Recommendation

- **If you want the title to match how you think:** Choose **Option B** (Framework-Grounded…) and keep "The Pyramid" as the name of the *framework* inside the article (e.g., "The Pyramid: four levels from foundational frameworks to executive briefing").
- **If you want maximum clarity and SEO for "technical writing" + "reuse" + "AI":** Keep **Option A**; use "framework-grounded writing" in the subtitle and first paragraph so your language is still front and center.
- **Hybrid:** **"Framework-Grounded Technical Writing: The Pyramid Approach to Decomposition, Reuse, and AI"** — your phrase first, Pyramid as the method name, key benefits in the subtitle.
