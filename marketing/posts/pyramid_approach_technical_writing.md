# The Pyramid Approach to Technical Writing - Marketing Content

## LinkedIn Post

I've created hundreds of strategy decks for different leaders. We moved slides from deck to deck, but we never connected them to a single source of truth. When someone asked "Why do we believe that?" or "Where's the backup?", there was rarely a path from the slide to the underlying thinking. Duplicated work, inconsistent messaging, and AI agents that hallucinate because they have nothing to ground in. Sound familiar?

Then I asked: What if we treated technical writing like software? Decompose it into layers. Build reusable components. Let humans and AI drill one layer deeper whenever they need detail.

The Pyramid Approach to Technical Writing: Decomposition, Reuse, and AI Grounding

A framework for organizing technical and product documentation in four levels, from foundational frameworks at the base to executive briefings at the top, so every piece is reusable and AI can tailor your ground truth to any audience or situation.

Key insights:
• Four levels mirror the C4 model: foundational frameworks (how you see the world) → artifacts (TDDs, PRDs) → strategy documents → executive briefing. Each tier is a reusable component.
• Outline top-down, write bottom-up. Define the deliverable structure first, then build at the leaves (frameworks, artifacts, strategy) and let your AI agent roll it up into the outline.
• When docs stack, "Where is the source of truth?" becomes a rhetorical question. Every slide traces back to strategy, then to artifacts, then to your foundational framework.
• This structure virtualizes your leadership strategy. Cursor, Copilot, and Slack AI can reason over your document hierarchy and answer complex questions from the same ground truth you use.

The result: I stopped rewriting the same story for every audience. New hires and AI agents can drill from a briefing down to the framework without me in the room.

The breakthrough wasn't writing more. It was organizing what we already had so the story we tell and the knowledge that powers it finally align. Technical writing is software when you apply the same decomposition and reuse.

**Description for Medium:**
A framework for making technical writing reusable, consistent, and useful for both humans and AI by applying software-engineering principles of decomposition to documentation. The Pyramid organizes content into four levels: foundational frameworks (how you see the world) at the base, then artifacts (TDDs, PRDs, C4 diagrams), then strategy documents, and executive briefings at the top. Each layer is a reusable component; readers and AI can drill one layer deeper whenever they need more detail. The approach emerged from a decade of creating hundreds of strategy decks in a meeting-driven culture where slides moved between decks but never connected to a single source of truth. The key shift: outline top-down (define the deliverable structure first) and write bottom-up (build at the leaves, then let AI roll components up into the outline). When the pyramid is in place, leadership strategy becomes virtualized. Agents like Cursor, Copilot, and Slack AI can reason over your document structure and answer complex questions from the same ground truth. The article includes the four-level framework, a worked example, six failure modes to avoid, and how to know when the Pyramid is working.
