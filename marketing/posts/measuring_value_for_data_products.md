# Measuring Value for Data Products - Marketing Content

## LinkedIn Post

"How do we justify investment in this data asset? How do we attribute value when the data is bundled into a larger offering and never sold on its own?" I've had these conversations with leaders and stakeholders throughout my career. The data product—scores, signals, pipelines—sits behind a wrapper customers actually pay for, and we end up arguing that "the data is what makes the product valuable" without a clear way to prove it. Sound familiar?

**Measuring Value for Data Products: A Three-Way Model**

A framework for product managers who own data products to assess value through the wrapper that exposes them, the internal use that consumes them, and the foundational qualities that make them worth building.

Key insights:
• Value lives in three places: external exposure (market and adoption of the wrapper), internal exposure (efficiency gains, cost-to-serve, risk mitigation), and foundational health (Discoverable, Actionable, Reliable, Governable, Ethical—plus adoption and OKR alignment)
• You need explicit attribution rules: TAM and adoption cap what you can claim; a logical rule for attributing bundle value back to the data product keeps the story defensible
• Internal exposure is measurable: compare workflows with and without the data product—hours saved, cost-to-serve reduction, correlation with outcomes—without waiting for a perfect experiment
• Foundational health drives adoption: the more consistently you deliver on DARE + adoption, the more users and teams depend on your data

Over the past two years I applied this exact model to Salesforce's Customer Success Score (CSS)—sizing Technical Health Score (THS) and Product Adoption Depth (PAD) through the market reach of their wrapper, tangible efficiency gains for Customer Success teams, and the reliability of the scores themselves. The result was a clear, defensible measurement grounded in explicit assumptions and reproducible math.

If you're a product manager owning a data product that hides behind a dashboard, a plan, or an API, this framework will help you have objective, reality-based conversations with finance, strategy, and engineering.
