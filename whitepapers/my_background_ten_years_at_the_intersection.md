## Where I Am Today: Customer Success Score and Technical Health

Since October 2023 I've been Senior Manager, AI Product Management at Salesforce, leading product and engineering for **Customer Success Score (CSS)**—Salesforce's 360° customer health signal. CSS is a customer-facing product distributed to every customer on a Customer Success plan: it demystifies Product Adoption, Expertise, and Technical Health so customers and our own teams can act on it.

My focus has been the **Technical Health Score (THS)** and the platform that powers it. I architected and built the end-to-end THS application—data value stream from lake through experience layer—aligned to Salesforce's Well-Architected Framework. The ML-driven score is now shared with roughly 85,000 customers as part of CSS. We cut Customer Success effort by more than half to diagnose and resolve implementation risks, and we've demonstrated strong predictive power: the score correlates with Sev1 incidents and cost-to-serve. We externalized it within 12 months, packaged it into Success Plans, and the work was recognized with the TSIA Customer Success Innovation award.

I was selected to lead a cross-functional product engineering group (PM, Data Science, Data Engineering, Analytics), managing a matrixed team that grew to 16 engineers, and was promoted to Product Engineering Lead (Group) within 12 months. On the platform side, I've led the consolidation of four disparate workstreams—product adoption breadth and depth, Technical Health Score, customer expertise—into a single service-oriented architecture on Snowflake, dbt, and Apache Airflow. That consolidation is estimated to reduce duplicate engineering by 50% or more and avoid millions in annual build and maintenance costs. I also architected and built the Product Feature Hierarchy (PFH), the taxonomy that maps all Salesforce product content and features to customer jobs-to-be-done, which enables our Product Adoption Depth score and the data science models we run for Sales Cloud and Service Cloud.

The business impact of CSS and THS shows up in attrition prevention, reduced cost-to-serve, and better health outcomes. Back-of-envelope, the data products and platform work I've been part of at Salesforce have contributed on the order of $100M+ in retained revenue, cost savings, and reduced cost-to-serve across the customer base.
